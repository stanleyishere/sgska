Quadruped Robot Design

November 2020 - August 2021

This project is a mandatory final year subject to partially fulfil the degree requirement. My project supervisor was Ir. Dr. Chua Bih Lii. Since I was actively invovled in making robots, I specifically chose quadruped robot as my project title to further enhance my knowledge and understanding of robotics.



Quadruped Robot

The quadruped robot is a five-bar linkage leg with direct drive mechanism.

Since most commercialized robots (Spot, AnyMAL, Laikago) are expensive due to sophisticated hardware to meet performance, I intended to make a cost-effective robot that approximated at $1000.

At the first phase, I did mathematical analysis of the leg linkage which aimed to find the relationship of leg tip and actuator.

For leg linkage arrangement, I proposed two arrangments.

In MATLAB, I tried to simulate the relationship between joint angles and leg tip coordinates using IK and also the joint coordinates during one step forward.

I also did FEA on certain robot parts to verify the design feasibility and functionality since supposedly they outght to be manufactured.

In this project, a universal bracket that could be mounted with motors and external encoders. The initial thickness was not accepted and FOS < 1. Therefore, further modification was implemented to increase the bracket thickness.

Nonethesless, due to severe pandemic situation in Kota Kinabalu, instead of fabricating the entire robot, I could only fabricate a single leg unit with the limited time I had in lab. Using 3D printing, I fabricated the leg joint to assemble all the linkages.
